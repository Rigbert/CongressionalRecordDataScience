But anyway, farm payments have come under increasing scrutiny, and that is legitimate because we are spending taxpayers' money. Critics of farm payments have argued that the largest corporate farms reap most of benefits from these payments. What is more, farm payments that were originally designed to benefit small and medium-sized family farmers have contributed to the demise of those smaller farmers as well because unlimited farm payments have placed upward pressure on land prices and cash trends and have contributed to overproduction and lower commodity prices, driving many family farmers off the farm.
