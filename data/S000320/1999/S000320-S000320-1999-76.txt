 Idaho, Alaska, and West Virginia have already each been granted flexibility under Section 1212(g) of TEA-21, to ``pool'' the state's obligation authority for high priority projects, as long as the total amount of funds authorized for any project for which the funds are allocated are not reduced. This flexibility does not have to be re-established legislatively on an annual basis, and nothing in the FY2000 Transportation Appropriations bill or report changes this flexibility.             supporting public lands discretionary project
