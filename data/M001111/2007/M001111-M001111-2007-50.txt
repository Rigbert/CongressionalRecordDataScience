 President, I ask unanimous consent that when the Senate completes its business today, it stand adjourned until 10 a.m., Thursday, November 1; that on Thursday, following the prayer and the pledge, the Journal of proceedings be approved to date, the morning hour be deemed to have expired, the time for the two leaders be reserved for their use later in the day; that there then be a period of morning business for 60 minutes, with Senators permitted to speak therein for up to 10 minutes each, with the time equally divided and controlled, with Republicans controlling the first half and the majority controlling the final half; that following morning business, the Senate resume consideration of the motion to proceed to H.R. 3963, the CHIP legislation; further, that all time consumed in morning business during today's session and tomorrow, as well as the time during the adjournment, count postcloture.
