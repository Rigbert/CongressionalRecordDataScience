Businesses in these communities rely on Canadian consumers to stay in business. Moreover, the impact on trade, including lumber and tourism, would extend beyond these communities and reverberate across Maine and through the Northern economy as a whole.  Those of us who represent states along the Canadian border know intimately how deep the shared ties between the U.S. and Canada truly are. Our relationship has included disagreements over the years, but our Canadian neighbors are part of our family--a fact that is literally and figuratively true for many Mainers whose extended families live across the border in Canada.
