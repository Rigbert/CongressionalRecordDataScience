 President, I want to speak for the fifth and probably final time--at least for a few days--on this subject of Department of Defense appropriations and the continuing program budget mismatch.  If Congress rolled back DOD's spending plans at the height of the cold war in the mid-1980's--and we did that on May 2, 1985--then why would Congress now move to pump up the defense budget when the cold war is over and the Soviet threat is gone? It makes no sense to me.  Mr. President, the General Accounting Office has prepared an interesting set of tables that portray the evolution of the future years defense program for the Defense Department and the budget mismatch with that future years plain. I ask unanimous consent to have this printed in the Record at this point.
