We have kept track and I believe every bank across America has said we are abandoning this approach, and it is a good thing. It is an indication to me that consumers across America have a much larger voice in this process today than they did even a few weeks ago.  Consumers and customers of major banks paid close attention when many of these banks, such as Bank of America, said they were going to charge these customers $5 a month to have access to their own money in their checking account. I was asked at the time: What should we do? I said: Customers of these banks should vote with their feet. Start looking for another bank. Find a bank or a credit union that treats them in the manner they want to be treated--fairly and respectfully.
