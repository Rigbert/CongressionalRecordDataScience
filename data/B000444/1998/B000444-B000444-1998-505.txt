 Even among them, there was no concern. As a matter of fact, there was a sense of bravado when they would say, ``obviously, this is no security threat to us, but. . . .'' The ``but'' would come in and the ``but'' always related to something along the lines of: This is an attempt on your part to isolate us, an attempt on your part to keep us from becoming full members of the economy to the West; or this is an attempt on your part to humiliate us, but not a security threat.  So I was speaking to the prospect of four nations, only three of which are being invited here. I was not talking about the Balts, Ukraine, Belarus, or other countries that could, theoretically, come up in a 2nd, 3rd, 5th, or 15th round.
