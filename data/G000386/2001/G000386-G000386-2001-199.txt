 I ask unanimous consent that, following that, on Wednesday, following the passage of H.R. 1836, there be 1 hour of morning business equally divided between the two leaders or their designees. I further ask consent that, following that time, the Senate then proceed to executive session and the Committee on Foreign Relations be discharged from further consideration of the nomination of Senator Howard Baker to be Ambassador to Japan. I further ask consent that the Senate then proceed to its consideration and there then be up to 2 hours for debate on the nomination, to be equally divided between the chairman and ranking member of the committee.  Finally, following the use or yielding back of time, that the Senate proceed to a vote on the nomination and, following that vote, the motion to reconsider be laid upon the table, the President be immediately notified of the Senate's action, and that the Senate then resume legislative session.
